Local Attractions Near Skaha Lakefront BnB

Skaha Lakefront BnB is surrounded by a wide range of attractions perfect for any type of visitor. Whether you’re a nature lover, wine connoisseur, or history buff, the Okanagan region has something for you. Discover unique landmarks, cultural experiences, and much more during your stay with us!

Okanagan Wine Region

The Okanagan Valley is one of Canada’s premier wine regions. Famous for its breathtaking vineyards and award-winning wineries, you can explore local favorites such as Red Rooster Winery, Blasted Church Vineyards, and Painted Rock Estate Winery. These wineries offer stunning views, educational tours, and tastings of some of the best wines in the country.

Historical Sites

Step back in time and explore the area's rich history. Visit the Okanagan Heritage Museum in nearby Penticton, which showcases the region’s indigenous and settler history, or check out the historic Okanagan Falls Railway Station, which offers a glimpse into the early days of the region’s development.

Skaha Lake

Beyond just a place for water sports, Skaha Lake is a local treasure. Enjoy its tranquil waters and sandy beaches, or take a peaceful walk along its shoreline to take in the stunning mountain views. You can even explore its surrounding parks, such as Skaha Bluffs Provincial Park, where you'll find local wildlife and rugged hiking trails.

Okanagan Falls Farmers' Market

Visit the Okanagan Falls Farmers' Market, a seasonal event where you can purchase fresh produce, handmade goods, and enjoy local delicacies. It’s a great way to experience the vibrant community culture of the Okanagan region.

Golfing with a View

Golf enthusiasts will enjoy the stunning courses around Okanagan Falls, including the St. Andrews by the Lake Golf Course, which offers sweeping views of the surrounding valley and vineyards. Perfect for a relaxing day on the green.

Scenic Drives & Tours

Take a leisurely drive along the Okanagan Scenic Drive to experience the stunning beauty of the valley. The route offers breathtaking views of orchards, vineyards, and the sparkling waters of Skaha and Okanagan Lakes. You can also take guided tours for more in-depth exploration of the region’s culture and history.
